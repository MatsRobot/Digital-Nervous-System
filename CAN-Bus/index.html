🏎️ CAN-Bus: Controller Area Network

CAN-Bus is the most robust communication protocol in the robot's architecture. It acts as a high-reliability backbone connecting the Intel N100 to distributed Motor Nodes across the machine.

Core Role: Synchronizing high-priority movement commands across all actuator modules while resisting electrical noise from the motors.

🔌 Protocol Logic: Multi-Master Robustness

CAN-Bus uses Differential Signaling (CAN High and Low). The receiver looks at the voltage difference between the two wires, allowing it to ignore noise that hits both wires simultaneously. It is a Multi-Master system; if two nodes talk at once, the one with the lowest ID wins through "Arbitration."

✅ Strengths

  • Noise Immunity: Bulletproof against motor interference.
  • Error Correction: Advanced hardware-level CRC checks.
  • Priority: Critical messages (E-Stop) always get through first.

⚠️ Weaknesses

  • Complexity: Requires specific transceivers (MCP2515).
  • Wiring: Must be a linear daisy-chain with termination.
  • Data Size: Limited to 8 bytes per message frame.

🧪 Experimental Design: Termination & Noise

Our implementation focuses on physical stability in a high-current environment:

  1. 120Ω Impedance Matching: We placed termination resistors at the physical ends of the bus to prevent signal "reflections" that cause data corruption.
  2. Twisted Pair Routing: We twisted the CAN_H and CAN_L wires together to maximize the noise-canceling effects of differential signaling.
  3. Priority Arbitration: We assigned the lowest ID (0x001) to the Emergency Stop system, ensuring it overrides all telemetry data on the bus.
